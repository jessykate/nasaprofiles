What is people.openNASA?

NASA has a contact directory that exposes basic information about it's employees. Both the internal and external versions of this contact directory expose administrative details about individuals such as contact information and departmental code.

Although this system helps us find contact information about those we already know, often we find ourselves wanting to search for people based on specific skills or attributes. When we find them, we would like to know a bit more about who are they and what they do.

people.openNASA is a profile system that makes use of the existing NASA contact directory, and gives employees the option to expand their profile with more information. people uses all the public information already available about each NASA employee. If you search here, you'll get the same results you would get by using people.nasa.gov-- and more.

We hope that this service will support the NASA community and our colleagues in finding and learning about one another, and collaborating more effectively.

So this is a NASA service?

No, this is not a NASA service. It was made with public information on personal time, by people who happen to be NASA employees.

This is a private, non-government website. Any material posted is done during off-duty, non-official time. The service itself is hosted on a private server and requires a NASA e-mail to edit profiles associated with each e-mail.

What Motivated This Project?

There are approximately 120,000 people in the U.S. Space Program, most of whom do not know each other. We provide a platform where employees can have a picture displayed, add in the current project they are working on, provide tags and skills, and list side projects.

Inspired by work in the open government movement, this service is an experiment in transparency and accessibility of the NASA workforce, for the NASA workforce. The developers of this service are committed to contributing to an open and honest workplace and allowing greater access within and into the U.S. space program.

This service has been desired for some time within NASA. It took the Sunlight Labs to provide leadership and encourage groups to host their own Great American Hackathon over the weekend of December 11-12, 2009. This project was started at that hackathon, and the first revision was released a month later.
